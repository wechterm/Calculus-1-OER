Example 2.5.27. Limit of a Composite Cosine Function.

Evaluate \(\limit_{x\to \pi/2}\cos \left(x-\frac{\pi}{2}\right).\)
🔗
Solution.
The given function is a composite of \(\cos x\) and \(x-\frac{\pi}{2}.\) Since \(\limit_{x\to \pi/2}\left(x-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)=0\) and \(\cos x\) is continuous at 0, we may apply the composite function theorem. Thus,
🔗
\begin{equation*} \limit_{x\to \pi/2}\cos \left(x-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)=\cos \left(\limit_{x\to \pi/2}\left(x-\frac{\pi}{2}\right)\right)=\cos (0)=1. \end{equation*}
