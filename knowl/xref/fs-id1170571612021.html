Example 2.4.16. Evaluating a Limit by Simplifying a Complex Fraction.

Evaluate \(\limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{\frac{1}{x+1}-\frac{1}{2}}{x-1}.\)
🔗
Solution.
  1. \(\ds\frac{\frac{1}{x+1}-\frac{1}{2}}{x-1}\) has the form \(0/0\) at 1.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. We simplify the algebraic fraction by multiplying by \(2(x+1)/2(x+1):\)
    \begin{equation*} \limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{\frac{1}{x+1}-\frac{1}{2}}{x-1}=\limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{\frac{1}{x+1}-\frac{1}{2}}{x-1}\cdot \frac{2(x+1)}{2(x+1)}. \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Next, we multiply through the numerators. Do not multiply the denominators because we want to be able to cancel the factor \((x-1)\text{ : }\)
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} =\limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{2-(x+1)}{2(x-1)}{(x+1)}. \end{equation*}
    Then, we simplify the numerator:
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} =\limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{-x+1}{2(x-1)(x+1)}. \end{equation*}
    Now we factor out -1 from the numerator:
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} =\limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{-(x-1)}{2(x-1)(x+1)}. \end{equation*}
    Then, we cancel the common factors of \((x-1)\text{ : }\)
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} =\limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{-1}{2(x+1)}. \end{equation*}
    🔗
  3. Last, we evaluate using the limit laws:
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \limit_{x \to 1 }\frac{-1}{2(x+1)}=-\frac{1}{4}. \end{equation*}
    🔗
