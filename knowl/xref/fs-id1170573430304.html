Theorem 2.5.23. Continuity of Polynomials and Rational Functions.

Polynomials and rational functions are continuous at every point in their domains.
🔗
🔗

Proof.

Previously, we showed that if \(p(x)\) and \(q(x)\) are polynomials, \(\limit_{x\to a}p(x)=p(a)\) for every polynomial \(p(x)\) and \(\limit_{x\to a}\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}=\frac{p(a)}{q(a)}\) as long as \(q(a)\neq 0.\) Therefore, polynomials and rational functions are continuous on their domains.
